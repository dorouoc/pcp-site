# LITERATURE REVIEW WORKFLOW

1. **Define Question**: Clearly articulate the research question or topic.
2. **Discover Papers**: Use `puma-research-tools` to find relevant sources.
3. **Read Papers (Three-Pass Method)**:
   - **Pass 1 (5-10 min)**: Skim title, abstract, headings, conclusion.
   - **Pass 2 (~1 hour)**: Read thoroughly; note contributions, methods, assumptions.
   - **Pass 3 (Deep)**: Re-read crucial parts; analyze proofs/experiments in detail.
4. **Take Notes**:
   - **Fleeting Note**: Quick capture of impressions.
   - **Literature Note** (`30-Resources`): For each paper, record title, summary, key findings, methods, and citations.
   - **Permanent Note** (`50-Zettelkasten`): Synthesize overarching insights or cross-cutting ideas.
5. **Synthesize**:
   - Identify patterns, gaps, and contradictions.
   - Generate new hypotheses or connections.
6. **Cite and Iterate**:
   - Include full citations (use Zotero keys, e.g. `[@doe2026]`).
   - Update Maps of Content (MOCs) to reflect new knowledge.

## OUTPUT

- **Literature Notes** in `30-Resources` with structured summaries (questions, methods, results).
- **Permanent Notes** in Zettelkasten with generalized insights.
- **Updated MOCs** for topics (e.g., agent architectures, prompt strategies).
